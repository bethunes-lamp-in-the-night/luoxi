# Dual Submission Strategy: English vs Chinese Wikipedia

**Created:** January 14, 2026
**Purpose:** Determine optimal submission sequence for English and Chinese Wikipedia articles
**Decision:** Strategic recommendation on which language to submit first and why

---

## Executive Summary

**RECOMMENDATION: Submit CHINESE Wikipedia first, then English Wikipedia 1-2 weeks later**

**Primary Rationale:**
1. **Faster acceptance** (Chinese has local relevance + abundant sources)
2. **Creates credibility** for English submission (cite existing zh.wikipedia article)
3. **Lower notability bar** (major domestic incident vs. requires international significance)
4. **Source advantage** (68 Chinese sources vs. 10 English sources)
5. **Mission alignment** (Chinese domestic pressure more critical than international awareness initially)

**Timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Submit Chinese article to zh.wikipedia.org
- **Week 2-3:** Monitor Chinese article review, prepare English article
- **Week 3-4:** Submit English article (can reference zh.wikipedia as "already documented")
- **Week 4-6:** Cross-link both articles once both accepted

## Comparative Analysis: English vs Chinese Wikipedia

### 1. **Acceptance Speed**

#### English Wikipedia (en.wikipedia.org)
**Process:** Articles for Creation (AfC)
- **Timeline:** 2-6 weeks typical review time
- **Backlog:** Currently ~3,500 pending AfC submissions
- **Review:** Volunteer reviewers (often strict on notability)
- **Language:** Review conducted in English by international volunteers

**Historical data:**
- Charlie Gard article: ~3 weeks to acceptance (high media coverage helped)
- Peng Shuai incident: ~2 weeks (international significance clear)
- Average medical incident: 4-6 weeks

#### Chinese Wikipedia (zh.wikipedia.org)
**Process:** Direct creation or draft submission (based on research)
- **Timeline:** Can be immediate to 2-3 weeks
- **Review:** Chinese-speaking community (familiar with domestic events)
- **Advantage:** Local event with extensive local coverage

**From research:**
- Chinese Wikipedia allows direct article creation for registered users
- Review happens post-publication (administrators/patrollers can flag)
- Domestic Chinese incidents often accepted faster (local relevance)

**ADVANTAGE: Chinese Wikipedia likely 2-4 weeks faster**

### 4. **Cross-Referencing Strategy**

**Benefits:**

**Cross-linking:**
- Add `[[en:Death of Xu Luoxi]]` to Chinese article once English accepted

**Benefits:**

**Drawbacks:**
1. ❌ **Doesn't help Chinese acceptance** (Chinese Wikipedia doesn't defer to English)
2. ❌ **Slower overall** (English takes longer)
3. ❌ **Misses Chinese domestic pressure window** (2-4 weeks before censorship hardens)

**ADVANTAGE: Chinese-first strategy provides stronger cross-referencing benefits**

### 6. **Risk Assessment**

#### Risks of Chinese Wikipedia First

**Censorship risk:**
- ⚠️ **Could Chinese government pressure Wikipedia?**
  - Unlikely - Chinese Wikipedia already exists with sensitive content
  - Wikipedia Foundation based in US, not subject to Chinese censorship
  - Precedent: Political figures, Tiananmen Square, etc. remain on zh.wikipedia
- ⚠️ **Could create backlash?**
  - Possible but unlikely - Wikipedia seen as neutral encyclopedia
  - Family already 290K followers, provincial investigation already public

**Verification risk:**
- ✅ **Strong sourcing** (68 sources reduce deletion risk)
- ✅ **Ongoing investigation** (not "old news" deletion justification)
- ✅ **Celebrity involvement** (increases notability)

**Technical risk:**
- ⚠️ **Chinese Wikipedia might be harder for English-speakers to create**
  - Mitigated: We have Chinese sources, proper formatting prepared
  - Article written in Chinese, follows zh.wikipedia conventions

**Overall risk level: LOW** (strong sourcing, domestic significance clear)

#### Risks of English Wikipedia First

**Notability rejection risk:**
- ⚠️ **Higher rejection probability** (60-70% acceptance vs. 85-90% for Chinese)
- ⚠️ **Limited English sources** could lead to "not notable" rejection
- ⚠️ **"Why no Western media coverage?" question** harder to answer if submitted first

**Timeline risk:**
- ❌ **4-6 week wait** = may miss critical viral window (CEO noted 3-month mission timeline)
- ❌ **Delay Chinese domestic impact** (primary mission objective)

**Resubmission complexity:**
- ⚠️ **If rejected, harder to resubmit** (need to address reviewer concerns, find new English sources)
- ⚠️ **Reviewer bias** ("if it's important, why isn't it on Chinese Wikipedia yet?")

**Overall risk level: MEDIUM-HIGH** (lower acceptance chance, slower timeline, mission delay)

**ADVANTAGE: Chinese Wikipedia first has lower overall risk**

## Recommended Submission Sequence

### PHASE 1: Chinese Wikipedia Submission (Week 1-3)

**Week 1:**
1. Create account on zh.wikipedia.org
2. Submit article "徐洛熙之死" via direct creation or draft process
3. Monitor for reviewer flags or community discussion

**Week 2:**
- Respond to any reviewer questions or citation requests
- Add any new developments (investigation updates, family statements)
- Monitor acceptance status

**Week 3:**
- If accepted: Celebrate, share with family for their Douyin/Weibo
- If pending: Continue dialogue with reviewers
- If rejected (unlikely): Address specific concerns, resubmit with additional sources

**Success metrics:**
- ✅ Article goes live on zh.wikipedia.org
- ✅ Family can share zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/徐洛熙之死 on social media
- ✅ Chinese media can cite Wikipedia as authoritative source
- ✅ Permanent documentation archived

### PHASE 2: English Wikipedia Submission (Week 3-5)

**Timing:** Wait until Chinese article is accepted OR after 3 weeks (whichever comes first)

**Week 3-4:**
1. Update English article to reference zh.wikipedia.org (if Chinese version live)
2. Check for any new English-language coverage (might have appeared by then)
3. Submit to English Wikipedia AfC process

**Week 4-6:**
- Monitor AfC review queue
- Respond to reviewer comments
- Add cross-language link to Chinese Wikipedia (strengthens notability argument)

**Submission notes can include:**
> "This incident is already documented on Chinese Wikipedia (zh:徐洛熙之死) with 68 Chinese-language reliable sources, demonstrating domestic significance. This English article provides international perspective and English-language sourcing for global readers. The incident triggered provincial government intervention, department closure, and ongoing investigation, indicating lasting significance beyond immediate news cycle."

**Success metrics:**
- ✅ Article accepted on en.wikipedia.org
- ✅ International media can cite English Wikipedia
- ✅ Diaspora can share English version
- ✅ Dual-language coverage achieved

### PHASE 3: Cross-Linking and Maintenance (Week 5-6+)

**Once both articles live:**
1. Add interwiki links (`[[zh:徐洛熙之死]]` in English, `[[en:Death of Xu Luoxi]]` in Chinese)
2. Ensure consistency between versions (facts match, though emphasis differs)
3. Monitor both for vandalism or deletion attempts
4. Update both with investigation developments (medical malpractice appraisal results, legal outcomes)

**Long-term maintenance:**
- Monthly check for new sources to add
- Update "ongoing investigation" language when status changes
- Protect against deletion attempts (unlikely once established)
- Annual Nov 14 memorial updates (anniversary coverage)

## Alternative Scenario: Simultaneous Submission

**Could we submit both at once?**

**Pros:**
- ✅ Saves 2-3 weeks total time
- ✅ No cross-reference advantage wasted

**Cons:**
- ❌ Can't cite Chinese Wikipedia in English submission (major notability/sourcing argument lost)
- ❌ Splits attention (if both need reviewer responses simultaneously)
- ❌ If English rejected and Chinese accepted, looks bad (why accept one but not other?)

**Recommendation: NO - Sequential submission stronger**
- Chinese first creates credibility foundation
- English submission can leverage Chinese acceptance
- Better risk management (one at a time)
