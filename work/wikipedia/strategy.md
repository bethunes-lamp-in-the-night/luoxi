## NPOV (Neutral Point of View) Compliance Strategy

### Wikipedia's Core Content Policy

From WP:NPOV:
- "Representing fairly, proportionately, and as far as possible without editorial bias, all significant views that have been published by reliable sources"
- "Articles should not take sides, but should explain the sides"

### Applying NPOV to Luoxi Case

#### Challenge 1: The Evidence Appears Damning

**The Facts:**
- Autopsy contradicts hospital's diagnosis
- Patch left inside heart despite records saying "removed"
- 6.5cm wound unsutured
- Hospital deleted official statement

**Wikipedia Problem:** This sounds like clear-cut malpractice/fraud, BUT Wikipedia cannot draw that conclusion without reliable sources explicitly stating it.

**NPOV Solution:**

‚ùå **Avoid (POV):**
"The hospital murdered baby Luoxi through fraudulent diagnosis and gross negligence."

‚úÖ **Use (NPOV):**
"The autopsy report, as reported by multiple news sources, found discrepancies between the hospital's diagnosis and the forensic findings. The hospital subsequently closed its entire pediatric cardiothoracic surgery department and officials were disciplined pending investigation."

#### Challenge 2: Family vs. Hospital Perspectives

**Wikipedia Requirement:** Present both sides fairly, even if one side is less credible.

**Current Status:**
- **Family perspective:** Extensively documented (290K Douyin followers, multiple interviews)
- **Hospital perspective:** One deleted statement (December 20, 2025), otherwise silent

**NPOV Solution:**

‚úÖ **Balanced presentation:**
"The family rejected the hospital's initial compensation offer, with the father stating 'We don't want money, we only want the truth.'<ref>[Citation]</ref> The hospital released a statement on December 20, 2025, defending the surgical indication, but deleted the statement within hours.<ref>[CDT Archive]</ref> The hospital declined to comment further to media inquiries.<ref>[News article if available]</ref>"

**Note:** Even if hospital's position seems weak, Wikipedia must present it neutrally. Deleting the statement is itself a fact that can be reported neutrally.

#### Challenge 3: Censorship Angle

**Wikipedia Problem:** Saying "Chinese government censored this story" can be seen as POV if not carefully sourced.

**NPOV Solution:**

‚ùå **Avoid (POV):**
"The Chinese Communist Party attempted to suppress the story through widespread censorship."

‚úÖ **Use (NPOV):**
"Multiple articles about the incident were removed from Chinese social media platforms. China Digital Times, which tracks internet censorship in China, documented at least four articles that were deleted after initial publication.<ref>[CDT]</ref> Despite censorship efforts, the mother's Douyin account reached 292,769 followers as of [date].<ref>[Citation]</ref>"

### Undue Weight Analysis

**WP:UNDUE:** "Articles should not give minority views or aspects the same amount of coverage as more widely held views."

**Applying to Luoxi:**

**Mainstream view (widely reported):**
- Baby died after surgery
- Autopsy findings differ from hospital diagnosis
- Department was closed
- Provincial investigation underway

**Minority/fringe views (less verification):**
- 5+ other children died at same hospital (NTD, unverified)
- Hospital performed unnecessary surgeries for profit (theory, not proven)
- Investigators were bribed (speculation)

**NPOV Solution:**
- **Lead section:** Focus on mainstream established facts
- **Body:** Can mention theories/speculation with attribution ("Some observers have speculated that..." or "Unverified reports suggest...") but give less space than confirmed facts
- **Do not include:** Totally unsubstantiated claims even with attribution

### Tone Guidelines

Wikipedia favors **clinical, encyclopedic tone** over emotional/advocacy language:

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "Heartbreaking tragedy"
- "Innocent victim"
- "Corrupt hospital system"
- "Brave parents fighting for justice"
- Exclamation points!

‚úÖ **Use:**
- "The incident"
- "Five-month-old infant"
- "Allegations of malpractice"
- "The family's campaign for accountability"
- Neutral punctuation.

**Exception:** Direct quotes can be emotional (Han Hong: "This case was wrong in such an outrageous way!" is fine as a quote with attribution).

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

**Likelihood:** MEDIUM - Controversial Chinese topics often face deletion challenges

**Reasons articles get deleted:**
- "Not notable" - Insufficient independent sources
- "Systemic bias" - Over-reliance on one country's sources

**Mitigation:**

- **Engage WikiProject Medicine** - Get expert editors to review pre-submission
- **Prepare AfD defense** - List all sources, precedent articles (Peng Shuai, vaccine scandal)

**If nominated for deletion:**
- Participate in AfD discussion with policy arguments, not emotional appeals
- Cite sources demonstrating notability
- Reference precedent articles with similar source profiles
- Request "Keep" votes from neutral editors at WikiProject Medicine, WikiProject China

### Risk 2: NPOV Disputes / Edit Wars

**Likelihood:** HIGH - Emotionally charged cases attract POV editing

**Scenarios:**
- Family supporters add advocacy language ("murdered baby," "corrupt hospital")
- Hospital defenders remove critical information or add victim-blaming
- Political editors frame as "CCP censorship" vs. "Western propaganda"
- Medical professionals debate surgical necessity in article (belongs on Talk page)

**Mitigation:**
- **Semi-protect article** after creation (prevents anonymous/new users from editing)
- **Monitor closely** first 2-4 weeks after publication
- **Revert POV edits** with edit summary citing WP:NPOV
- **Engage on Talk page** - Move disputes to discussion, not main article
- **Request admin intervention** if edit warring persists (WP:ANI)

**Best practice:** Establish article with strong sourcing and neutral tone from start. First version sets precedent.

### Risk 3: Copyright / Privacy Violations

**Likelihood:** MEDIUM - Family photos, medical records are sensitive

**Concerns:**
- **Photos:** Family owns copyright. Cannot upload without permission.
- **Medical records:** Privacy laws may prohibit publication even if family consents
- **Personal information:** Parent names, addresses, contact info (per company handbook)

**Mitigation:**
- **Do NOT upload photos** unless family explicitly donates to Wikimedia Commons with appropriate license
- **Do NOT reproduce medical records** verbatim - cite news articles summarizing them
- **Do NOT include** parent names, home address, or other PII (unless family has made fully public)
- **Request permission** from family if using any images - offer to help them license properly

**Wikipedia policy:** Better to have NO images than improperly licensed images.

### Risk 4: Politically Motivated Editing from CCP-aligned Editors

**Likelihood:** MEDIUM-HIGH - Chinese government has history of Wikipedia editing

- Downplaying censorship evidence
- Adding "context" that hospital followed procedures

**Mitigation:**

- **Assume good faith initially** - Engage in Talk page discussion
- **Document patterns** - If same editor repeatedly removes sourced content, report to WP:ANI
- **Invoke WP:MEAT** - If suspicious coordinated editing occurs
- **Request page protection** if disruption persists

**Critical:** Wikipedia policy is on our side IF we have sources and neutrality. Politically motivated editors often overreach and get blocked.

### Risk 5: Story Fades Before Wikipedia Readiness

**Likelihood:** MEDIUM - News cycles move fast

**Scenario:** By time international sources are secured (6-8 weeks), public interest has waned and article seems dated.
