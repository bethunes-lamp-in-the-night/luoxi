### Challenge #5: Evidence Tier Preservation

**Challenge:** Chinese sources mix verified facts, allegations, and speculation without always clearly distinguishing.

**Solution:** Applied rigorous evidence tier system:
- **Tier 1 (Verified):** Multiple sources OR official documents OR photo/video evidence
- **Tier 2 (Strongly Supported):** Credible single source requiring attribution
- **Tier 3 (Allegations):** Family claims requiring "alleges"/"claims" language
- **Tier 4 (Speculation):** Avoid or clearly mark

**Example:**
- "Hospital falsely diagnosed" = Tier 3 Allegation (family's interpretation)
- "Autopsy found no coronary sinus defect" = Tier 2 Strongly Supported (official autopsy but published by family)
- "Baby died November 14, 22:03" = Tier 1 Verified (official death certificate)

**Approach:**
- Assigned evidence tier to each claim
- Applied appropriate language (state as fact vs. attribute to source vs. mark as allegation)
- Provided sourcing for verification
- Flagged any uncertain tier assignments
